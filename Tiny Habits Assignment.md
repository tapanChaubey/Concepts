# 2. Tiny Habits by BJ Fogg - Core Message

## Question 2
### How can you use B = MAP to make making new habits easier? What are M, A and P?

BJ Fogg explains behavior using the formula:

### B = MAP

Where:

1. **M = Motivation**  
   The desire or willingness to do something.

2. **A = Ability**  
   How easy the habit is to perform.

3. **P = Prompt**  
   A reminder or trigger that tells you to do the habit.

### How to Use It
To build new habits successfully:
- Keep the habit very simple and easy.
- Use a clear reminder or trigger.
- Do the habit even when motivation is low.

Example:
After brushing my teeth (Prompt), I will do 5 pushups (Ability), and slowly build consistency.

# 4. Book Summary of Atomic Habits

## Question 5
### What is the book's perspective about Identity?

The book explains that lasting habits come from changing identity instead of only focusing on goals. A person should believe in becoming the type of person who naturally performs the habit.

Example:
Instead of saying “I want to run,” say “I am a healthy and disciplined person.”

## Question 6
### Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit easier to do?

1. Start with very small actions.
2. Reduce friction and obstacles.
3. Prepare the environment in advance.
4. Use clear cues and reminders.
5. Make the habit simple and convenient.
6. Practice consistently at the same time or place.
7. Focus on repetition instead of perfection.

# 5. Reflection

## Question 8
### Pick one habit that you would like to do more of? What are the steps that you can take to make the cue obvious or the habit more attractive or easy and or response satisfying?

### Habit I Want to Build:
Daily coding practice.

### Steps:
1. Set a fixed coding time every day.
2. Keep laptop and coding platform ready before starting.
3. Start with only 20–30 minutes daily.
4. Track progress using a checklist or app.
5. Practice interesting coding problems.
6. Celebrate completion by appreciating small progress.
7. Study with friends sometimes to make it enjoyable.

## Question 9
### Pick one habit that you would like to eliminate or do less of? What are the steps that you can take to make the cue invisible or the process unattractive or hard or the response unsatisfying?

### Habit I Want to Reduce:
Excessive social media usage.

### Steps:
1. Turn off unnecessary notifications.
2. Remove social media apps from the home screen.
3. Use app timers or blockers during study hours.
4. Keep the phone away while working.
5. Replace scrolling time with productive activities.
6. Avoid using the phone immediately after waking up.
7. Track daily screen time and reduce it gradually.
