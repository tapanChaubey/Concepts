# Listening and Active Communication

---

## 1. Active Listening

**Question 1: What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening? (Minimum 6 points)**

1. Give the speaker your full attention and remove all distractions
2. Maintain eye contact to show you are present
3. Don't interrupt while the other person is speaking
4. Use body language like nodding to show you are following
5. Ask questions if something is not clear
6. Summarize or repeat back what you heard to confirm you understood correctly

---

## 2. Reflective Listening

**Question 2: According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening? (Write in your own words, use simple English)**

- Try to understand what the other person is feeling, not just what they are saying
- Reflect those feelings back to them so they feel heard
- Don't jump in with your own opinion or advice right away
- Paraphrase what they said and check if you got it right
- The whole point is to make the other person feel understood before you respond

---

## 3. Reflection

**Question 3: What are the obstacles in your listening process?**

- I get distracted by my phone
- Sometimes I start thinking about what I want to say while the other person is still talking
- If the topic feels boring I lose focus
- When I'm stressed or tired I don't listen properly

**Question 4: What can you do to improve your listening?**

- Keep my phone away during conversations
- Focus on the person speaking instead of planning my reply
- Take notes so I don't miss important things
- Remind myself to be patient and let the person finish

---

## 4. Types of Communication

**Question 5: When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day to day life?**

- When talking to someone senior like a manager or teacher, even if I disagree I stay quiet
- In a group where I feel my opinion won't matter I just go along with whatever is decided
- When I want to avoid conflict I just say yes even if I don't mean it

**Question 6: When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?**

- When I'm really frustrated and feel like nobody is listening to me
- When someone repeats the same mistake again and again
- When I'm under a lot of pressure and things are not working out

**Question 7: When do you switch into Passive Aggressive (sarcasm/gossiping/taunts/silent treatment and others) communication styles in your day to day life?**

- When I'm upset with someone I give them the silent treatment instead of talking about it
- Sometimes I use sarcasm to show I'm annoyed without directly saying it
- I agree in the moment but then don't follow through on what was said
- I talk about the issue with someone else instead of going directly to the person involved

**Question 8: How can you make your communication assertive? You can watch and analyse the videos, then think what would be a few steps you can apply in your own life?**

- Say what you feel directly using "I feel" or "I think" instead of blaming the other person
- Be honest about what you want or need without hinting at it
- Learn to say no without feeling guilty about it
- Stay calm even when the conversation gets difficult, don't raise your voice
- Don't keep things bottled up, address it before it becomes a bigger issue
- Listen to the other person's side too, assertive doesn't mean ignoring them, it means being fair to both sides
